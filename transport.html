Getting Around

Trams in Portugal have generally been traditional electric cars in large cities such as Lisbon, Porto and Sintra. Portuguese tram routes are typically, but not necessarily, tourist attractions.
Take a relaxing train ride through the lush Braga hills to the exciting cities of Lisbon and Porto and connect with Portugal's distinctive, rich, and beautiful heritage.
In Portugal's significant tourism industry, buses are undoubtedly often utilised for day trips, getting to and from airports, and other local transportation needs.
On a sightseeing tour by tuk tuk, you'll see some of the hills of Lisbon without working up a sweat. Let your driver be your guide as you explore some of the city's typical neighborhoods.
For getting around in Portugal, we can use public transport because the nation's trains, trams, buses, taxis, and funiculars are hygienic, effective, and easy to use. Over 86 million rail passengers in Portugal travelled 2.2 billion kilometres in 2020, while another 8 billion kilometres were covered by tram and bus passengers, according to the International Union of Railways.


For the public transportation payment method in Lisbon, we can use the Viva Viagem card. A Viva Viagem is a card with an embedded chip. A quick and easy way to pay for public transport in Lisbon. You need to buy a Viva Viagem card to use the metro, ferries and S-Bahn. On buses, trams, and cable cars, you can use your Viva Viagem card or buy tickets from the more expensive drivers if you don't have one. This Viva Viem card is the most recommended for tourists.
