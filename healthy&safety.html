The good news is that Portugal is frequently ranked as one of Europe's safest travel destinations. In fact, it came in third place overall among 163 nations on the Global Peace Index. The index is calculated by taking into account variables including the crime rate, terrorist attacks, violent protests, political climate, and international relations. Portugal does well in each of these areas. One of the reasons Portugal is regarded as safe is that its firearms regulations are not lightly enforced. Portugal experiences few violent crimes, and when they do, they typically don't involve tourists. Even so, every traveller should take the standard safety precautions and stay in frequented areas.

so, here we include the common crime and Covid-19 Measurement in Portugal.

Most Common Crime in Portugal

Portugal is a place that will leave you speechless. It continues to be one of the most favoured travel locations in Europe despite its fame.

This reputation is a result of the area's wild beauty, vibrant metropolitan environment, and diverse culture. Robbery is the type of crime that affects tourists the most frequently in Portugal.

There is no need to become overly concerned about it because it is not a very common worry. In 2018, there were only 102.8 robberies per 100,000 people in the nation.

Ways to avoid it?

  1. Leave spare cash and valuables in a safe place.
  2. Avoid leaving items in an unattended car, even for a short period; if you have no alternative, hide them in the boot before you reach your destination


PRO TIP!

Remember that foreign-registered and hire cars are often targeted by thieves.

Covid-19 Measurement in Portugal

To enter in Portugal (including Azores and Madeira), it is no longer required a proof of a negative COVID test or to present a COVID-EU digital certificate or vaccination or recovery certificate issued by third countries, accepted or recognized in Portugal.

Measures in force may be reviewed in accordance to the evolution of the pandemic.

IMPORTANT: Before traveling, it is important to confirm the entry formalities and procedures with the airline company and the Portuguese border service - SEF (www.sef.pt / gricrp.cc@sef.pt), the entity responsible for the border control. Please be advised that you should arrive at the airport beforehand and take into account the necessary procedures for domestic and international flights. If you're traveling during peak times like early morning, evening or summer, or during holidays and other events, please plan accordingly and allow for extra time.
