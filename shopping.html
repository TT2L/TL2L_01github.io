Things You Should Buy While on A Trip to Portugal

No trip to Portugal would be complete without purchasing the ideal souvenir to remember this amazing nation. There are plenty of authentic and meaningful Portugal souvenirs to select from. While it's all too
tempting to visit the nearest souvenir shop, it's best to avoid tourist traps, especially if you want to buy traditional and Portuguese handicrafts and food products.

Cork Products

Did you know that Portugal is the world's largest cork producer? You will not only see cork tree plantations as you travel through the Alentejo countryside, but you will also be able to tour them and learn more about cork production in Portugal.

This very adaptable material has long been utilised to create a wide range of inventive and functional objects. A genuine cork handicraft piece offers a wonderful long-lasting and ecological Portugal souvenir.

Where to buy authentic cork products

Lisbon
- Cork & Co
- House of Cork
Porto
-Cultura Portuguesa
Portuguese Ceramics

Pottery has been cultivated for generations in Portugal, with locations such as Alentejo and Coimbra housing artisanal enterprises and pottery museums, and Evora famed for its rustic clay pots. Earthenware plates and bowls, as well as beautiful sculptures and souvenirs made from local clay, are immensely popular.

Where to buy authentic Portuguese ceramics

Lisbon
- Feira da Ladra flea market
- Cerâmicas na Linha
- A Vida Portuguesa
- Armazem das Caldas
Porto
- Vista Alegre
- Bordallo Pinheiro
Barcelos Rooster

The Barcelos Rooster (Galo de Barcelos) is Portugal's national symbol, and it can be found on anything from woodwork to embroidery. The best rendition is a ceramic Barcelos Rooster which has been colourfully hand-painted with decorative patterns. The traditional Galo is available in a variety of shapes and sizes, ranging from tiny trinkets to large statuettes.

The rooster picture originated in a famous folktale from the city of Barcelos and has come to represent the Portuguese love for life, making this a great souvenir to remember your trip and the amor da vida Portugal tends to inspire.

Portuguese Embroidery & Linens

Portugal has a rich legacy of elaborate embroidery, stretching from the island of Madeira to Viana do Castelo in the northeast.

With finely embroidered motifs and patterns, artisans produce lovely tableware, clothes,scarves, and accessories. Colorful stripes and Barcelos Roosters (more on those soon!) are two common icons you'll notice repeatedly.

Where to buy authentic Portuguese ceramics

Lisbon
- A Vida Portuguesa
Filigree (Filigrana) Jewellery

Filigree is one of the world's oldest jewelry-making processes, having been used in Portugal since at least the early 16th century. Goldsmiths twist and braid small metal threads to produce exquisite lace-like designs that are often embellished with semi-precious stones.

Where to buy authentic filigree jewellery

Lisbon
- Joalharia Do Carmo
- Gomes & Gois
- Bohème Lisboa

Shopping in Portugal

Mercado de Loulé

It's a vibrant market with a variety of freshly caught fish, fruits, nuts, honey, jams, home-made products, and items to take home in hand-painted ceramics. It's vibrant and buzzing with locals.

Under The Cover
